💊 Pharmacokinetics Essentials
Zero-order kineticsFixed amount eliminated (phenytoin, ethanol, aspirin high dose)
First-order kineticsFixed fraction eliminated (most drugs)
Bioavailability (F)Fraction reaching systemic circulation unchanged
High first-pass metabolismMorphine, propranolol, lignocaine, nitrates, labetalol
Vd >1 L/kgWidely distributed; lipophilic (chloroquine, digoxin)
Plasma protein binding (highest)Warfarin (99%), Diazepam (98%)
CYP450 inducersRIPES: Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Phenytoin, Ethanol (chronic), Smokng/St John's wort
CYP450 inhibitorsSICKFACES: Sulfonamides, Isoniazid, Cimetidine, Ketoconazole, Fluconazole, Amiodarone, Ciprofloxacin, Erythromycin, SSRIs
Therapeutic index = TD50 / ED50 (high TI = safer drug)

📊 Biostatistics Essentials
SensitivityTP/(TP+FN) — ability to detect disease (rule OUT)
SpecificityTN/(TN+FP) — ability to exclude disease (rule IN)
PPVTP/(TP+FP) — depends on prevalence (↑prevalence → ↑PPV)
NPVTN/(TN+FN)
IncidenceNew cases / at-risk population per time period
PrevalenceAll cases / total population at a point in time
Prevalence ≈Incidence × Duration (for steady-state chronic disease)
Relative risk (RR)Cohort studies; RR=1 → no association
Odds ratio (OR)Case-control studies; approximates RR when prevalence low
p-value <0.05Statistically significant result
Type I error (α)False positive; reject true null hypothesis (p <0.05)
Type II error (β)False negative; fail to reject false null hypothesis
SnNout = High Sensitivity → Negative result rules OUT disease
SpPin = High Specificity → Positive result rules IN disease
📈 Study Designs
RCT (gold standard)Randomised controlled trial; least bias; prospective
Systematic review / meta-analysisHighest level of evidence
Cohort studyProspective; exposure → outcome; RR calculated
Case-control studyRetrospective; outcome → exposure; OR calculated
Cross-sectional studyPrevalence; snapshot in time; no temporality
Ecologic studyGroup-level data; risk of ecologic fallacy
Bias (selection)Non-random sampling; Berkson's bias
Recall biasCase-control studies; cases recall better than controls
ConfoundingThird variable associated with both exposure and outcome
Randomisation controls forKnown + unknown confounders
