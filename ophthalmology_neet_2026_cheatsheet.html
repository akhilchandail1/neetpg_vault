👁️ Ocular Anatomy — Key Numbers
Axial length of eye (emmetropic)24 mm
Corneal diameter11–12 mm (horizontal); 10–11 mm (vertical)
Corneal thickness (central)0.5–0.55 mm (500–550 µm)
Corneal refractive power+43 D (contributes most to total refraction)
Total refractive power of eye+60 D
Crystalline lens power+20 D (varies with accommodation)
Fovea centralis — location3.5 mm temporal + 0.5 mm inferior to optic disc
Foveola (fovea floor)0.35 mm; only cones; maximum visual acuity
Optic disc diameter1.5 mm; no photoreceptors (blind spot)
Normal cup:disc ratio≤0.4; asymmetry >0.2 = suspicious
IOP normal range10–21 mmHg (Goldmann applanation)
🔭 Refractive Errors
Myopia (short-sightedness)Image falls anterior to retina; long axial length; concave (–ve) lens corrects
Hypermetropia (long-sightedness)Image falls posterior to retina; short axial length; convex (+ve) lens corrects
AstigmatismUnequal curvature of cornea; cylindrical lens corrects
PresbyopiaLoss of accommodation; onset ~40–45 yrs; reading glasses (+ve lens)
Anisometropia>2.5 D difference between eyes; amblyopia risk
Amblyopia — definitionReduced vision despite optical correction; critical period <7–8 yrs; Rx: patch better eye
High myopia — riskRetinal detachment, POAG, CNVM, vitreous degeneration; >–6 D
LASIK — principleExcimer laser reshapes corneal stroma; flap raised with microkeratome
LASIK contraindicationKeratoconus, thin cornea (<500 µm), progressive myopia, dry eye

🔬 Layers of Cornea & Retina
Corneal layers (anterior→posterior)Epithelium → Bowman's → Stroma → Descemet's → Endothelium
Corneal endothelium — specialCannot regenerate (post-mitotic); critical cell density >1000 cells/mm²
Bowman's layerCannot regenerate if destroyed → scarring
Descemet's membraneCan regenerate; thickens with age
Retinal layers (vitreous→choroid)ILM → Nerve Fibre → Ganglion cell → IPL → INL → OPL → ONL → IS/OS → RPE
Blood-retinal barrier (inner)Retinal capillary endothelium (tight junctions)
Blood-retinal barrier (outer)RPE (retinal pigment epithelium) tight junctions
Macula — definition5 mm area with >2 layers of ganglion cells; temporal to disc

Convex lens — image (beyond F)Real, inverted, variable size (converging)
Plane mirror — imageVirtual, erect, same size, behind mirror
Concave mirror — imageReal inverted (beyond C); virtual erect (within F)

Glaucoma Disorders
POAG, PACG, secondary glaucoma, medical and surgical management
🔵 Primary Open-Angle Glaucoma (POAG)
MC type worldwidePOAG (chronic simple glaucoma)
Aqueous outflow obstructionTrabecular meshwork dysfunction (angle is OPEN)
PresentationAsymptomatic (insidious); painless; peripheral field loss first
IOP in POAGUsually >21 mmHg but 1/3 have normal-tension glaucoma
Risk factorsAge, family history, African descent, high myopia, thin cornea, disc hemorrhage
Early field defectBjerrüm scotoma (arcuate) → nasal step → tubular vision → blindness
Cup:disc ratio significance>0.6 or asymmetry >0.2 = suspicious; vertical elongation of cup
ISNT ruleNormal: Inferior > Superior > Nasal > Temporal rim; reversal = glaucoma
Gold standard for IOPGoldmann applanation tonometry
1st line treatmentProstaglandin analogue (latanoprost, bimatoprost) — once daily, most effective
⚡ Acute Angle Closure Glaucoma (PACG)
MechanismPupil block → iris pushed forward → angle closes → aqueous outflow blocked
Risk factorsHypermetropia, shallow AC, female, Asian, age, dark room / stress (pupil dilation)
Classic presentationSudden severe eye pain + headache + nausea/vomiting + halos around lights + blurred vision
SignsCorneal oedema (steamy); mid-dilated fixed pupil; very high IOP (>50 mmHg); ciliary flush
AC depthVery shallow anterior chamber
Immediate treatmentIV acetazolamide 500 mg + topical timolol + pilocarpine 2% + IV mannitol
Definitive treatmentLaser peripheral iridotomy (YAG-PI) in BOTH eyes
Plateau iris syndromeAngle closure despite patent PI; peripheral iris obstructs angle; Rx: laser iridoplasty

🔬 Secondary Glaucomas
Neovascular glaucoma (NVG)New vessels on iris/angle (rubeosis iridis); PDR, CRVO, OIS; 100-day glaucoma after CRVO
NVG treatmentAnti-VEGF (bevacizumab) + pan-retinal photocoagulation + trabeculectomy/drainage implant
Pigmentary glaucomaIris pigment deposits on trabecular meshwork; Krukenberg spindle (corneal endothelium); exercise ↑ IOP
Pseudoexfoliation (PXF) glaucomaMC identifiable cause of secondary OAG; dandruff-like deposits on lens; weak zonules; risk of subluxation
Steroid-induced glaucomaTopical > periocular > systemic; trabecular meshwork dysfunction; appears 3–6 weeks post-steroids
Phacolytic glaucomaHypermature cataract → lens proteins leak → macrophage-clogged trabeculum; OAG
Phacomorphic glaucomaSwollen intumescent lens → secondary angle-closure; PACG mechanism
Congenital glaucoma (buphthalmos)Trabeculodysgenesis; Haab's striae (horizontal breaks in Descemet's); Rx: goniotomy / trabeculotomy
🔧 Glaucoma Surgery
TrabeculectomyGold standard filtration surgery; creates fistula + bleb; antimetabolites (MMC, 5-FU) to prevent scarring
Laser trabeculoplasty (SLT/ALT)Burns trabecular meshwork → ↑ outflow; POAG; SLT = selective (repeatable)
Glaucoma drainage devicesAhmed valve, Molteno implant, Baerveldt; for failed trabeculectomy or NVG
Cycloablation (cyclophotocoagulation)Destroys ciliary body → ↓ aqueous production; end-stage / refractory glaucoma
Post-trabeculectomy complicationsHypotony + flat AC; bleb failure (fibrosis); endophthalmitis (blebitis); cataract

ECCE (Extracapsular)Nucleus expressed whole; large incision (10–12 mm); posterior capsule preserved for IOL

PCO (Posterior capsule opacification)MC late complication; Elschnig's pearls (lens epithelial cells); Rx: YAG laser capsulotomy

Endophthalmitis treatmentIntravitreal vancomycin + ceftazidime; vitrectomy if severe

Retina & Vitreous
Diabetic retinopathy, ARMD, RVO, RD, retinoblastoma
🩸 Diabetic Retinopathy (DR)
Earliest changeMicroaneurysms (earliest ophthalmoscopic sign)
NPDR featuresMicroaneurysms, dot-blot haemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton wool spots, venous beading/looping
4-2-1 rule (severe NPDR)4 quadrant haemorrhages OR 2 quadrant venous beading OR 1 quadrant IRMA → PDR within 1 year (50%)
PDR — high-risk characteristics (HRC)NVD ≥1/3 disc area OR any NVD with vitreous haemorrhage OR NVE ≥1/2 disc area with VH
Clinically Significant Macular Oedema (CSMO)Hard exudate within 500 µm of fovea; any retinal thickening within 500 µm; zone ≥1 disc area within 1 DD of fovea
CSMO treatmentIntravitreal anti-VEGF (1st line); focal laser (2nd line)
PDR treatmentPan-retinal photocoagulation (PRP / scatter laser)
Vitreous haemorrhage in PDRIntravitreal anti-VEGF + pars plana vitrectomy (PPV)
Ghost cell glaucomaOld VH → degenerated RBCs (ghost cells) → trabecular blockage
👁️ Age-Related Macular Degeneration (ARMD)
DrusenDeposits between Bruch's membrane and RPE; early/intermediate ARMD
Dry (non-exudative) ARMD85–90%; geographic atrophy (central RPE loss); gradual vision loss
Wet (exudative) ARMD10–15%; CNV (choroidal neovascularisation); rapid severe vision loss; subretinal fluid/blood
Amsler gridDetects metamorphopsia (distorted vision) — key symptom of wet ARMD
Gold standard diagnosisOCT (optical coherence tomography); FFA for CNV characterisation
Wet ARMD treatmentIntravitreal anti-VEGF (ranibizumab, bevacizumab, aflibercept) — mainstay
Dry ARMD — preventionAREDS2 formula: vitamin C + E + lutein/zeaxanthin + zinc (reduces progression)
Disciform scarEnd-stage wet ARMD; fibrovascular scar replaces macula; severe irreversible loss
🔴 Retinal Vascular Occlusions
CRVO — appearance"Blood and thunder" fundus: flame haemorrhages in all 4 quadrants; disc oedema; tortuous veins
CRVO — typesIschaemic (NVG risk 40% — 100-day glaucoma) vs Non-ischaemic
BRVO — MC siteSuperotemporal branch (MC); AV crossing → flame haemorrhages in sector
CRVO treatmentIntravitreal anti-VEGF / dexamethasone implant (Ozurdex); treat NVG with PRP
CRAO — appearancePale oedematous retina + cherry red spot at fovea (choroidal circulation intact)
Cherry red spot — causesCRAO, Tay-Sachs disease, Niemann-Pick disease, Gaucher's, CO poisoning
CRAO treatment (within 90 min)IOP reduction, ocular massage, carbogen, AC paracentesis; thrombolysis if within 4.5 hrs
Hollenhorst plaqueCholesterol embolus at bifurcation; glistening yellow; carotid artery source
🔺 Retinal Detachment (RD)
Rhegmatogenous RD (RRD)MC type; break/tear in retina → fluid entry; painless flashes + floaters + curtain shadow
RRD — risk factorsHigh myopia, aphakia, trauma, lattice degeneration, post-cataract surgery
Traction RD (TRD)Fibrovascular membrane pulls retina; PDR (MC cause), trauma, PVR
Exudative RDFluid under retina; no break; choroidal tumour, VKH, Coats' disease; shifting fluid
Lattice degenerationPeripheral retinal thinning + white lines (lattice); snail-track; RRD risk
RD surgery — pneumatic retinopexyGas bubble + laser; superior breaks; office procedure
Scleral bucklingSilicone band/sponge to indent sclera; closes break; RRD
Pars plana vitrectomy (PPV)Gold standard for complex RD, TRD, PVR; removes vitreous; tamponade with gas/silicone oil
Macula-on vs macula-off RDMacula-off = urgent surgery within 24 hrs; each day delay → worse prognosis
🔴 Retinoblastoma
MC intraocular tumour in childrenRetinoblastoma
MC presentationLeukocoria (white pupillary reflex / "cat's eye" reflex)
2nd MC presentationStrabismus (squint)
Gene mutationRB1 gene (chromosome 13q14); tumour suppressor; "two-hit hypothesis" (Knudson)
Bilateral retinoblastomaAlways hereditary; germline mutation; risk of trilateral RB (pinealoblastoma)
Unilateral15% hereditary; 85% sporadic somatic mutation
Flexner-Wintersteiner rosettesPathognomonic of retinoblastoma on histology
Calcification on imagingCalcified mass on CT/B-scan; pathognomonic feature
International Classification (IIRC)Groups A–E; Group E = enucleation; Groups A–D = globe-salvage therapies
TreatmentEnucleation (large tumour); intra-arterial chemotherapy (IAC); focal therapy (laser, cryotherapy)

🧠 Optic Nerve Disorders
Optic neuritisPainful vision loss; RAPD +ve; dyschromatopsia (red desaturation); associated with MS (50%)
Optic neuritis — treatmentIV methylprednisolone (speeds recovery but doesn't improve final VA)
AION (Anterior Ischaemic Optic Neuropathy)Sudden painless visual loss; altitudinal field defect; swollen pale disc; no pain

🏆 High-Yield Mnemonics
Corneal layers — "EABSDE": Epithelium, Anterior limiting (Bowman's), Bowman's, Stroma, Descemet's (posterior limiting), Endothelium
ISNT rule (glaucoma): In a normal disc, Inferior rim > Superior rim > Nasal rim > Temporal rim. Violation of ISNT = glaucomatous rim loss
Cherry red spot causes — "TGCNO": Tay-Sachs, Gaucher's, CRAO, Niemann-Pick, Other (CO poisoning)
Pupil-sparing CN III: Medical (diabetic/ischaemic) CN III palsy SPARES pupil — blood vessels penetrate from outside in; ischaemia hits core motor fibres first, spares outer pupillomotor fibres
Horner's syndrome — "PAM": Ptosis (partial), Anhidrosis, Miosis — plus apparent enophthalmos and upside ptosis (lower lid elevation)
Vitamin A deficiency stages: Night blindness → Conjunctival xerosis → Bitot's spots → Corneal xerosis → Keratomalacia (emergency) → Corneal scarring

🔥 Exam Traps & Frequently Confused
Papilloedema vs papillitisPapilloedema = bilateral, ↑ICP, vision preserved early, no RAPD; Papillitis = unilateral, RAPD, painful, vision lost
POAG vs PACGPOAG = painless, open angle, slow; PACG = painful acute, closed angle, emergency
Surgical vs medical CN III palsySurgical (compressive = aneurysm) = pupil INVOLVED; Medical (ischaemic/diabetic) = pupil SPARED
Chalazion vs hordeolumChalazion = chronic, painless, Meibomian, I&C; Hordeolum = acute, painful, infected, hot compresses
Dry ARMD vs wet ARMDDry = 85%, slow, geographic atrophy; Wet = 15%, fast, CNV, metamorphopsia, anti-VEGF treatment
Asteroid hyalosis vs synchysis scintillansAsteroid = calcium-phosphate, unilateral, stays suspended, benign; Synchysis = cholesterol, settles, pathological
Argyll Robertson vs Adie's pupilBoth = light-near dissociation (near > light); AR = small irregular bilateral (syphilis); Adie's = large, slow, unilateral, benign
Rubella cataract — do NOT operate earlyLive virus persists in lens for months; operation releases virus → endophthalmitis
