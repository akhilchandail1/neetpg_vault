📋 DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria — Schizophrenia
≥2 of: Delusions, Hallucinations, Disorganised speech, Disorganised/catatonic behaviour, Negative symptoms (at least 1 must be from first 3) Duration: ≥6 months (≥1 month active symptoms) Significant social/occupational dysfunction
ICD-11 duration requirement≥1 month of Sx (shorter than DSM)
Schizophreniform disorderSx 1-6 months; same criteria as schizophrenia
Brief psychotic disorderSx <1 month; full recovery; triggered by stress
Schizoaffective disorderSchizophrenia + prominent mood episode; mood present >50% of total duration
Delusional disorderNon-bizarre delusions ≥1 month; functioning relatively preserved; NO hallucinations
Age of onsetMen: 18-25 yrs; Women: 25-35 yrs (bimodal)

🧠 Neurobiology of Schizophrenia
Dopamine hypothesis↑ mesolimbic D2 → positive Sx; ↓ mesocortical D1/D2 → negative Sx
Mesolimbic pathwayVTA → nucleus accumbens; positive symptoms; reward
Mesocortical pathwayVTA → prefrontal cortex; negative symptoms; cognition
Glutamate hypothesis↓ NMDA receptor activity → mimics schizophrenia (PCP, ketamine)
Serotonin hypothesis↑ 5-HT2A activity → negative/cognitive Sx; ATAPs block 5-HT2A
Brain changes↑ ventricular size; ↓ hippocampal/temporal lobe volume; ↓ frontal activity (hypofrontality)
GeneticsConcordance: MZ twins ~50%; DZ ~15%; sibling ~10%; general population ~1%
Dopamine pathways (4)Mesolimbic, Mesocortical, Nigrostriatal (EPS), Tuberoinfundibular (↑ prolactin)

😔 Major Depressive Episode — DSM-5 Criteria
Mnemonic: SIG E CAPS Sleep ↓ or ↑ | Interest ↓ (anhedonia) | Guilt / worthlessness Energy ↓ | Concentration ↓ | Appetite ↓ or ↑ | Psychomotor agitation/retardation | Suicidal ideation / death wishes
Duration≥2 weeks; ≥5 symptoms; ≥1 must be depressed mood OR anhedonia
Dysthymia (Persistent Depressive Disorder)Depressed mood ≥2 years (adults); ≥1 year (children); subthreshold Sx
Double depressionMDD superimposed on dysthymia
Seasonal affective disorderWinter depression; hypersomnia, hyperphagia; light therapy
Atypical depression featuresMood reactivity + hypersomnia + hyperphagia + leaden paralysis + rejection sensitivity; MAOIs best
Melancholic depressionAnhedonia, diurnal variation (worse AM), psychomotor Δ; responds to TCAs/ECT
🎭 Manic Episode — DSM-5 Criteria
Mnemonic: DIG FAST Distractibility | Insomnia (decreased need for sleep) | Grandiosity Flight of ideas | Activity ↑ (goal-directed) | Speech (pressured) Thoughtlessness (risky behaviour)
Duration of mania≥7 days (or any duration if hospitalised); ≥3 of 7 symptoms (4 if mood is only irritable)
HypomaniaSame criteria but ≥4 days; NOT severe enough to impair function or cause hospitalisation; NO psychosis
Mixed episode (DSM-5)≥3 symptoms of opposite pole during manic/depressive episode (mixed features specifier)
Elation vs EuphoriaElation = infectious; Euphoria = empty, hollow; Ecstasy = extreme
Secondary mania causesSteroids, L-DOPA, antidepressants, hyperthyroidism, SLE, TBI, right frontal lesion
🔄 Bipolar Disorders — Classification
TypeEpisodesKey Feature
Bipolar I≥1 full manic episode (with or without depression)Most severe; psychosis may occur; hospitalisation common
Bipolar IIHypomanic + Major depressive episodes; NO full maniaOften misdiagnosed as MDD; antidepressants alone can trigger switch
CyclothymiaHypomania + subthreshold depression ≥2 yearsNever symptom-free >2 months; 15-50% progress to Bipolar I/II

⚡ ECT — Electroconvulsive Therapy
Absolute indicationsSevere depression with suicidal risk, psychotic depression, catatonia, treatment-resistant depression, NMS
First-line indicationSevere depression in pregnancy (safer than drugs)
MechanismGeneralised seizure; ↑ neurotrophic factors; ↓ HPA axis; monoamine changes
Sessions (standard course)6-12 sessions; 3×/week (bilateral); 2×/week (unilateral)
Electrode placementBilateral (BL) vs Right unilateral (RUL); BL more effective; RUL fewer cognitive SE
Side effectsRetrograde + anterograde amnesia (transient); confusion; headache; muscle pain
Only absolute contraindication↑ Intracranial pressure (ICP)
Relative contraindicationsRecent MI, space-occupying lesion, pheochromocytoma, aortic aneurysm
Pre-ECT workupECG, EEG, CBP, XRay spine, anaesthetic assessment; informed consent
⚠️ Suicide — Risk Assessment
SAD PERSONS Scale (risk factors): Sex (male), Age (<19 or >45), Depression, Previous attempt, Ethanol/substance use, Rational thinking loss, Social support ↓, Organised plan, No spouse, Sickness (chronic illness)
MC method (worldwide)Hanging (high lethality); women → poisoning
Highest risk disorderSchizophrenia > Bipolar > MDD (but MDD has highest absolute numbers)
High-risk periods in depressionEarly morning, early recovery phase (energy returns before mood)
Suicidal ideation typesPassive (wish to die) → Active (plan) → Intent → Attempt
Drug reducing suicide riskLithium (best evidence) > Clozapine (schizophrenia) > SSRIs
ParasuicideDeliberate self-harm without intent to die; manipulative; personality disorder
Shneidman's triadPsychological pain + Perturbation + Lethality

😰 Panic Disorder
Panic attack = Discrete period of intense fear/discomfort: ≥4 of 13 symptoms, peaking within 10 minutes STUDENTS FEAR the 3 Cs: Sweating, Trembling, Unsteady, Depersonalisation, Excessive HR, Numbness, Tingling, Shortness of breath | Feeling of dying, Chest pain, Abdominal distress, Rigors, Chills
Panic disorder diagnosisRecurrent unexpected panic attacks + anticipatory anxiety ≥1 month
AgoraphobiaFear of open/public places; often secondary to panic disorder
First-line pharmacotherapySSRIs (sertraline, escitalopram); SNRIs; avoid antidepressants precipitating anxiety early
First-line psychotherapyCBT (most effective long-term)
Biological challenge testsSodium lactate infusion, CO2 inhalation, yohimbine → can precipitate panic attack
NeurobiologyLocus coeruleus (noradrenergic); amygdala; parabrachial nucleus
🔄 OCD — Obsessive Compulsive Disorder
ObsessionIntrusive, ego-dystonic, recurrent thought/image/urge; patient resists
CompulsionRepetitive behaviour/mental act to neutralise obsession; ego-dystonic
Ego-syntonic vs Ego-dystonicOCD = ego-dystonic; Obsessive personality (OCPD) = ego-syntonic
MC obsessionContamination → washing compulsion (MC overall)
MC compulsionChecking (doors, locks, gas) → doubt obsession
Y-BOCS scaleYale-Brown Obsessive Compulsive Scale; 0-40; gold standard severity tool
First-line pharmacotherapySSRIs (high dose, ≥12 weeks trial); clomipramine (most potent but SE profile)
First-line psychotherapyERP — Exposure Response Prevention (most evidence)
NeurobiologyOrbitofrontal-thalamo-striatal circuit hyperactivity; ↑ serotonin deficiency
OCD-related disordersBDD, Hoarding, Trichotillomania, Excoriation (skin picking), Body dysmorphic disorder
😱 PTSD & Acute Stress Disorder
PTSD diagnostic clusters (DSM-5): BEAN B — Re-experiencing (flashbacks, nightmares, intrusions) E — Avoidance (stimuli, thoughts related to trauma) A — Alterations in cognition/mood (negative beliefs, guilt, anhedonia) N — Arousal/Reactivity ↑ (hypervigilance, sleep, irritability, reckless)
PTSD onsetSymptoms >1 month after trauma; can have delayed expression (≥6 months)
Acute Stress DisorderSame Sx but 3 days–1 month after trauma; dissociation prominent
First-line pharmacotherapy PTSDSSRIs (sertraline, paroxetine — FDA approved); venlafaxine
Prazosin in PTSDAlpha-1 blocker; reduces nightmares specifically
First-line psychotherapyTrauma-focused CBT; EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing)
Debriefing (CISD)Critical Incident Stress Debriefing — NOT recommended (may worsen PTSD)

💉 Opioids — Intoxication, Withdrawal, Treatment

NOT life-threatening (unlike alcohol)Opioid withdrawal is extremely uncomfortable but not fatal in healthy adults

🚨 Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)
FEVER mnemonic: Fever (hyperthermia >38°C), Encephalopathy, Vitals unstable (tachycardia, BP lability), Elevated CPK + rigidity, Rigidity (lead-pipe)
PathophysiologySudden D2 blockade → hypothalamic temperature dysregulation + muscle rigidity
Lab findings↑ CPK (most important), leukocytosis, myoglobinuria, ↑ LFTs
ManagementSTOP antipsychotic → Supportive (cooling, hydration, monitoring) → Dantrolene + Bromocriptine
Dantrolene MOAMuscle relaxant; blocks Ca2+ release from SR; reduces rigidity and hyperthermia
Bromocriptine MOAD2 agonist; restores dopamine tone
ECT in NMSUsed if pharmacotherapy fails; effective
NMS vs Serotonin syndromeNMS: bradykinesia, lead-pipe rigidity, slow onset. SS: hyperreflexia, clonus, tremor, rapid onset
💊 Clozapine — Unique Properties
IndicationsTreatment-resistant schizophrenia; suicidality in schizophrenia; tardive dyskinesia
Treatment-resistant definitionFailure of ≥2 antipsychotics at adequate dose for ≥6 weeks each
MechanismWeak D2; strong D4, 5-HT2A, H1, M1, α1 blockade; unique receptor profile
Agranulocytosis risk1–2%; mandatory ANC monitoring: weekly (first 6 months) → biweekly → monthly
ANC threshold to stopANC <1500/mm³ → stop clozapine; ANC <1000 → never rechallenge
Other SE of clozapineHypersalivation (nocturnal), sedation, weight gain, metabolic syndrome, myocarditis, seizures (dose-related)
Most weight gainClozapine = Olanzapine > Risperidone > Quetiapine > Aripiprazole (least)
No prolactin elevationClozapine & Quetiapine — least prolactin elevation

🧒 ADHD — Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder
DSM-5: ≥6 inattentive OR ≥6 hyperactive-impulsive symptoms (≥5 if ≥17 yrs); present in ≥2 settings; onset before 12 yrs; persists ≥6 months; impairs function
