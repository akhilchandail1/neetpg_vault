Thanatology — Signs of Death
Postmortem changes, time since death estimation
🌡️ Postmortem Cooling (Algor Mortis)
Rate of cooling~1.5°F (0.8°C) per hour (first 6 hrs)
Body reaches room temp~24 hrs (in average conditions)
Henssge nomogramBest formula for time of death
Rectum site preferredFor core temp measurement
Factors ↑ cooling rateThin body, cold environment, windy, wet
Factors ↓ cooling rateObese, hot environment, clothed
🔵 Postmortem Lividity (Livor Mortis)
Appears30 min – 2 hrs after death
Well established6–12 hrs
Fixed (non-blanchable)8–12 hrs (blood in vessels)
ColourPurplish-red (deoxyHb)
Cherry-red lividityCO poisoning, CN poisoning, cold exposure
Brown lividityMethaemoglobinaemia (nitrites, aniline dyes)
Floater (drowning)Pink lividity (anterior surface)
Contact flatteningPale areas where body pressed against surface
💀 Rigor Mortis
Onset1–2 hrs after death (warm climate: 30 min)
Complete6–12 hrs
Passes off24–48 hrs (secondary flaccidity)
MechanismATP depletion → actin-myosin lock
Nysten's ruleJaw → neck → trunk → arms → legs (descending)
Cadaveric spasmInstantaneous rigor (intense emotion/exertion)
Heat stiffeningPugilistic (boxing) posture
Cold stiffeningFreezing (not true rigor)
Rigor in fetusIntrauterine fetal death (IUFD)
🧫 Decomposition & Putrefaction
First sign of putrefactionGreen discolouration of RIF (caecum — gut bacteria)
Timing (tropical climate)24–48 hrs after death
MarblingGreenish network on skin (dilated blood vessels)
Bloating3–5 days (gas formation)
AdipocereSaponification of fat; wet environment; months–years
MummificationDesiccation in hot/dry environment
Skeletonisation~1 year in tropical climate
Best preserved tissueUterus (last to putrefy)

Wounds & Injuries
Blunt force, sharp force, firearm injuries, hanging, drowning
🔪 Types of Wounds
AbrasionSuperficial scraping; epidermis only; direction evidence
Contusion (bruise)Blunt force; extravasation of blood
LacerationBlunt force → tearing; irregular edges, tissue bridges
Incised woundSharp instrument; clean edges; length > depth
Stab woundSharp; depth > length; minimal bleeding external
Chop woundHeavy sharp weapon; penetrates bone
Laceration vs incisedTissue bridges present → Laceration; absent → Incised
Hesitation cutsMultiple superficial incisions → suicidal cut

Yellow5–7 days (bilirubin)

Wandering bruiseTracks along fascial planes
Spectacle haematomaFracture base of skull (raccoon eyes)

🔫 Firearm Injuries
Contact woundStellate tear; blackening; muzzle imprint; inverted edges
Close range (<15 cm)Blackening + tattooing (unburnt powder)
Intermediate (15–60 cm)Tattooing only (no blackening)
Distant (>60 cm)Circular hole, no blackening/tattooing

Exit woundLarger, irregular, everted edges
Grease collarDirt/lubricant deposited at entry
Rifled vs smooth boreRifled → single bullet; Smooth → shotgun pellets
Wad in shotgunOnly within 3 metres — indicates range

Typical hangingKnot at occiput; runs up the neck obliquely
Atypical hangingKnot at side or front (partial suspension)
COD in hangingAsphyxia + venous obstruction + reflex cardiac arrest
Judicial hanging CODFracture dislocation of C2-C3 (hangman's fracture)
Ligature strangulationHorizontal mark; murder or suicide
Manual strangulationALWAYS homicidal; nail marks, petechiae
ThrottlingManual strangulation from front
MuggingStrangulation from behind (arm lock)
Tardieu's spotsPetechiae (subconjunctival, pleural) — asphyxia

General Toxicology
Principles, routes, diagnosis, antidotes, corrosive poisons
⚗️ Toxicology Principles
LD50 definitionDose lethal to 50% of test animals
Therapeutic indexTI = LD50 / ED50 (narrow TI = dangerous drug)
Narrow TI drugsDigoxin, Warfarin, Lithium, Phenytoin, Theophylline
MC route poisoning (India)Oral (ingestion)
MC poison (India)Organophosphates (agricultural)
Phase I reactionsOxidation, reduction, hydrolysis (CYP450)
Phase II reactionsConjugation (glucuronidation, sulfation)
Tolerance↑ dose needed for same effect
TachyphylaxisRapid tolerance within short time

🌿 Organophosphate (OP) Poisoning
MOAInhibit acetylcholinesterase → ACh accumulation
Muscarinic effects (DUMBELS)Defecation, Urination, Miosis, Bradycardia, Emesis, Lacrimation, Salivation
Nicotinic effectsMuscle weakness, fasciculations, paralysis
CNS effectsSeizures, coma

Pralidoxime (2-PAM)Reactivates AChE if given within 48 hrs
Intermediate syndrome24–96 hrs; proximal limb weakness; respiratory failure
