🏆 High-Yield Mnemonics
CSOM Unsafe (Atticoantral) vs Safe (Tubotympanic): Unsafe = "ABCDE" — Attic/posterior marginal perforation, Bone erosion, Cholesteatoma, Dangerous complications, Erosion requires surgery
Salivary Stone site: "Sub-WAY" — Submandibular gland = Wharton's duct (most stones); PARot = PARotid = Stensen's duct (less common)
Laryngeal cartilages (paired): "ACCA" — Arytenoid, Cuneiform, Corniculate, (Aryepiglottic is a fold, not cartilage)
Cholesteatoma complications — BOAF: Brain abscess, Ossicular erosion, Aural polyp, Facial palsy; also: labyrinthine fistula, meningitis, sigmoid sinus thrombosis
CHARGE syndrome: Coloboma, Heart defects, Atresia choanae, Retardation (growth), Genital abnormalities, Ear anomalies
Neck dissection levels — "Some Surgeons Obtain Money Passionately": Submental, Submandibular (I), Superior jugular (II), Mid jugular (III), Omohyoid (IV), Posterior triangle (V)
⚡ Must-Know Classifications
Chandler classification (orbital complications)I=Preseptal cellulitis, II=Orbital cellulitis, III=Subperiosteal abscess, IV=Orbital abscess, V=Cavernous sinus thrombosis
Bailey's classification (branchial cyst)I=Deep to SCM, II=Anterior to SCM (MC), III=Between carotid vessels, IV=Pharyngeal wall
Holmgren-Plester classification (otosclerosis surgery)Stapedotomy vs Stapedectomy
Cottle classification (DNS)5 areas of nasal septum (I–V from anterior to posterior)
House-Brackmann grade (facial nerve)I=Normal → VI=Total paralysis
Meyerhoff classification (cholesteatoma)Congenital vs acquired (primary vs secondary)
Schucknecht classification (presbyacusis)Sensory, Neural, Metabolic (strial), Mechanical (cochlear conductive)
Fisch classification (glomus tumour)A=Middle ear, B=Tympanomastoid, C=Infralabyrinthine, D=Intracranial
🔥 Exam Traps & Frequently Confused
Rinne –ve BUT SNHL (false negative)Profound unilateral SNHL — crossover to better ear via BC; Weber lateralises to BETTER ear
Cholesteatoma — SAFE vs UNSAFETubotympanic (central perf) = SAFE; Atticoantral (attic/marginal perf) = UNSAFE
Acoustic neuroma — nerve of originVestibular nerve (NOT cochlear/acoustic nerve) — hence better called vestibular schwannoma
Epiglottitis — do NOT examine throatRisk of complete airway obstruction; secure airway first in theatre
JNA — do NOT biopsyExtremely vascular; life-threatening haemorrhage
Amoxicillin + EBV90% develop maculopapular rash — do not give; use macrolide instead
Parotid mass + facial nerve palsyMalignant until proven otherwise
Button battery — nasal/earRemove IMMEDIATELY; liquefactive necrosis within 1–2 hours
