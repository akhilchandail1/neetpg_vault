Basics & Morphology
Skin structure, primary and secondary lesions, dermatological signs
🔬 Skin Layers — High Yield
Epidermis layers (deep→superficial)BSSGL: Basale, Spinosum, Granulosum, Lucidum, Corneum
Stratum lucidum — whereOnly in thick skin (palms and soles)
Melanocytes — layerStratum basale (basal layer)
Langerhans cells — layerStratum spinosum (prickle cell layer); antigen-presenting cells
Merkel cells — functionMechanoreceptors (light touch); stratum basale
Desmosomes — locationStratum spinosum; cell-cell adhesion; pemphigus target
Hemidesmosomes — locationBasement membrane zone; epidermis-dermis adhesion; bullous pemphigoid target
Collagen in dermisType I (80%) + Type III (reticular fibers)
Rete ridges (pegs)Epidermal projections downward; interdigitate with dermal papillae
📋 Primary Lesions
MaculeFlat colour change <1 cm (patch if >1 cm)
PapuleRaised solid <1 cm (plaque if >1 cm)
NoduleRaised solid >1 cm; deeper than papule
VesicleFluid-filled <1 cm (bulla if >1 cm)
PustulePus-filled (may be sterile — psoriasis)
Wheal (urticaria)Transient oedematous papule/plaque; evanescent (<24 hrs)
TelangiectasiaDilated superficial capillaries; blanches on pressure
PurpuraNon-blanchable red/purple; >3 mm = ecchymosis; <3 mm = petechiae
ComedonePlugged follicular opening; open (blackhead) / closed (whitehead)
📋 Secondary Lesions & Special Terms
ScaleFlaky dead stratum corneum (psoriasis, tinea)
Crust (scab)Dried serum/blood/pus (impetigo — golden crust)
ErosionPartial-thickness epidermal loss; heals without scar
UlcerFull-thickness epidermal + dermal loss; heals with scar
ExcoriationScratch marks (linear erosions)
LichenificationThickened skin with exaggerated markings; chronic rubbing
FissureLinear crack through epidermis ± dermis (tinea pedis, eczema)
SinusBlind-ending tract (hidradenitis suppurativa)
BurrowSerpiginous tunnel in stratum corneum — scabies (pathognomonic)
🔑 Key Dermatological Signs
Auspitz signPinpoint bleeding on removing psoriasis scale
Nikolsky signLateral pressure → skin slides/peels (pemphigus vulgaris, SSSS, TEN)
Darier's signStroking → urticarial wheal; mastocytosis (urticaria pigmentosa)
Koebner phenomenonNew lesions at site of trauma; psoriasis, lichen planus, vitiligo, warts
Reverse KoebnerLesions CLEAR at trauma site; pityriasis rosea
PathergyExaggerated response to minor trauma; Behçet's disease
DermographismStroking → raised red line; MC form of physical urticaria
Wickham's striaeWhite lacy lines on lichen planus papules (reticulate pattern)
Leser-Trélat signSudden ↑ in seborrhoeic keratoses → internal malignancy (GI cancer)
Carpet tack signScaling in discoid lupus erythematosus (DLE)

Pautrier's microabscessesAtypical lymphocytes in epidermis — mycosis fungoides (CTCL)
🌡️ Drug Reactions — Patterns
Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS)<10% BSA epidermal detachment; mucosa involved; Nikolsky +ve
TEN (Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis)>30% BSA; SJS-TEN overlap = 10–30%; mortality 30–50%
MC cause SJS/TENDrugs: sulfonamides, allopurinol, carbamazepine, lamotrigine, NSAIDs
DRESS syndromeDrug Rash + Eosinophilia + Systemic Symptoms; onset 2–6 weeks; carbamazepine, allopurinol, dapsone
Fixed drug eruptionSame site recurs with re-exposure; hyperpigmented plaque; NSAIDs, tetracycline, cotrimoxazole
Photosensitivity drugsTetracyclines, thiazides, quinolones, amiodarone, psoralens
Drug-induced lupusHydralazine, procainamide, isoniazid; anti-histone antibody
Acneiform eruptionSteroids, lithium, INH, phenytoin, EGFR inhibitors

Bacterial, viral, fungal, and parasitic skin infections
🦠 Bacterial Skin Infections
Impetigo — MC organismS. aureus (bullous); S. pyogenes (non-bullous/crusted)
Impetigo — classic lesionHoney-golden crust; face around nose/mouth
Impetigo — complicationPost-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (NOT rheumatic fever)
Impetigo treatmentTopical mupirocin; oral flucloxacillin / cefalexin if widespread
EcthymaDeeper version of impetigo; ulceration; Group A strep
ErysipelasSuperficial dermis + lymphatics; sharp border; S. pyogenes; face/legs
CellulitisDeeper dermis + subcutis; diffuse border; S. aureus + S. pyogenes
ErythrasmaCorynebacterium minutissimum; coral-red fluorescence on Wood's lamp; axillae/groin
SSSS (Staphylococcal Scalded Skin Syndrome)Exfoliatin toxin (phage group II S. aureus); children; split at granular layer; Nikolsky +ve
SSSS vs TEN — biopsy levelSSSS = subgranular (superficial); TEN = subepidermal (deep)

Inflammatory Dermatoses
Eczema, psoriasis, lichen planus, acne, rosacea, urticaria
🔴 Psoriasis
MC typeChronic plaque psoriasis (psoriasis vulgaris)
Classic lesionWell-demarcated erythematous plaque with silvery-white scale; extensor surfaces + scalp
Auspitz signPinpoint bleeding on removing scale (dilated papillary vessels)
Candle grease / wax signScale removal resembles scraping candle
Nail changesPitting (MC), onycholysis, oil drop sign, subungual hyperkeratosis
HistologyAcanthosis + parakeratosis + Munro's microabscesses + dilated tortuous capillaries
Psoriatic arthritis30%; DIP joint; pencil-in-cup deformity; asymmetric; RF negative
Guttate psoriasisPost-streptococcal; raindrop lesions; young adults
Erythrodermic psoriasis>90% BSA; life-threatening; temperature dysregulation; high output HF
Biologics for psoriasisAnti-TNF (adalimumab), Anti-IL17 (secukinumab), Anti-IL23 (guselkumab)
🌊 Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)
Atopic triadEczema + Asthma + Allergic rhinitis
Gene defect in ADFilaggrin (FLG) mutation → impaired skin barrier
Infantile AD (<2 yrs)Face, scalp, extensor surfaces; weepy/crusted
Childhood AD (2–12 yrs)Flexural surfaces (antecubital/popliteal fossa); lichenification
Adult ADFlexures, hands, eyelids, neck; excoriations + lichenification
Dennie-Morgan foldsExtra infraorbital skin folds (Dennie–Morgan lines); AD feature
Treatment — mildEmollients + topical corticosteroids (low potency)
Topical calcineurin inhibitorsTacrolimus / pimecrolimus; face & flexures; steroid-sparing
DupilumabAnti-IL-4Rα (IL-4 + IL-13 blocker); moderate–severe AD; biologic
💜 Lichen Planus
Classic description — 6 PsPruritic, Purple, Polygonal, Planar, Papule, Plaque
MC siteFlexor wrists, ankles, lower back; also oral mucosa
Wickham's striaeWhite lacy network on surface of papules (pathognomonic)
Oral LPWhite reticular/erosive patches; buccal mucosa; pre-malignant (erosive type)
HistologyBand-like lymphocytic infiltrate at DEJ; hypergranulosis; saw-tooth rete ridges; Civatte bodies (colloid bodies)
Koebner phenomenonPresent in lichen planus
AssociationHCV infection (lichen planus — screen for hepatitis C)
TreatmentTopical corticosteroids (1st line); systemic steroids for widespread
🌺 Acne Vulgaris
PathogenesisSebum ↑ + follicular plugging + C. acnes + inflammation
C. acnes roleCutibacterium acnes (formerly Propionibacterium acnes)
Comedonal acne (Grade I)Open + closed comedones; topical retinoids (adapalene)
Mild-moderate (Grade II–III)Papulopustular; topical BPO + antibiotic OR oral antibiotic
Severe (Grade IV) / nodulocysticOral isotretinoin (vitamin A derivative; gold standard)
Isotretinoin — contraindicationPregnancy (teratogenic: cleft palate, CNS malformations); iPLEDGE program
Isotretinoin — side effectsCheilitis, dry skin, hypertriglyceridaemia, night blindness, depression, hepatotoxicity
Oral antibiotics for acneDoxycycline / lymecycline (max 3–6 months)
Acne conglobataSevere nodulocystic; interconnecting sinuses; males; isotretinoin
🌹 Rosacea & Urticaria
Rosacea — subtypesErythematotelangiectatic, Papulopustular, Phymatous (rhinophyma), Ocular
RhinophymaEnlarged bulbous sebaceous nose; Phymatous rosacea; males
Rosacea — treatmentTopical metronidazole / azelaic acid; oral doxycycline; laser for telangiectasia
Rosacea vs AcneRosacea: no comedones; flushing; central face; >30 yrs; triggered by sun/alcohol/spicy food
Acute urticaria<6 weeks; IgE-mediated (Type I); allergen triggered
Chronic urticaria>6 weeks; often idiopathic; anti-FcεRI antibodies
Urticaria treatmentNon-sedating antihistamines (cetirizine); omalizumab for refractory

Bullous Disorders
Autoimmune blistering diseases — pemphigus, pemphigoid, dermatitis herpetiformis
💥 Pemphigus Vulgaris
Target antigenDesmoglein 3 (+ Desmoglein 1 in mucocutaneous type)
AntibodyIgG anti-desmoglein
Level of splitSuprabasal (intraepidermal) — just above basal layer
Nikolsky signPOSITIVE — lateral pressure → extension of blister
Clinical featureFlaccid bullae → erosions; mucosal involvement (oral MC — 60% present first)
HistologyAcantholysis; Tzanck cells (rounded acantholytic keratinocytes); "tombstone" basal cells
DIF (Direct Immunofluorescence)IgG + C3 — fish-net / chicken-wire pattern (intercellular)
TreatmentHigh-dose oral prednisolone + rituximab (anti-CD20) or azathioprine/MMF
MC cause of death (before steroids)Sepsis (skin barrier loss); now steroid complications
🫧 Bullous Pemphigoid
Target antigenBP180 (collagen XVII) + BP230 — hemidesmosomes
AntibodyIgG anti-BP180 / anti-BP230
Level of splitSubepidermal (dermis-epidermis junction)
Nikolsky signNEGATIVE (tense bullae; roof intact)
Clinical featureElderly (>60 yrs); tense bullae on erythematous/urticarial base; flexures; pruriginous; mucosa spared (usually)
DIFLinear IgG + C3 at BMZ (basement membrane zone)
TreatmentPotent topical steroids (clobetasol) / oral prednisolone; doxycycline + nicotinamide (mild)
Pemphigoid gestationisPregnancy; periumbilical; same target (BP180); resolves postpartum; neonatal bullae

PathologyAutoimmune destruction of melanocytes; T-cell mediated
AppearanceChalk-white (depigmented) macules/patches; Wood's lamp: brilliant white fluorescence
MC typeNon-segmental (generalised) vitiligo

🏆 High-Yield Mnemonics
6 Ps of Lichen Planus: Pruritic, Purple, Polygonal, Planar, Papule, Plaque — on Wickham's striae
Psoriasis signs — "ACM": Auspitz sign (pinpoint bleeding), Candle-grease sign (scale scraping), Marcus-Munro microabscesses (neutrophils in corneum)
Koebner phenomenon diseases — "WLVP": Warts, Lichen planus, Vitiligo, Psoriasis (also molluscum, lichen sclerosus)
Pemphigus vs Pemphigoid memory aid: Pemphigus = Vulgaris = Flaccid = Suprabasalar = Positive Nikolsky = mucosa INVOLVED | Pemphigoid = Old = Tense = Subepidermal = Negative Nikolsky = mucosa SPARED
DRESS drugs — "CAVE": Carbamazepine, Allopurinol, Vancomycin, (anti-)Epileptics (phenytoin, lamotrigine, dapsone)
Leprosy reactions: Type 1 = Reversal = CMI increase = TT→BT→BL = Steroids | Type 2 = ENL = Immune complex = BL→LL = Thalidomide / Clofazimine

🔥 Exam Traps & Frequently Confused
Erysipelas vs CellulitisErysipelas = sharp border + raised + lymphatics; Cellulitis = diffuse border + deeper
SSSS vs TENSSSS = children + subgranular split + mucosa spared; TEN = adults + subepidermal + mucosa involved
Pemphigus foliaceus vs vulgarisFoliaceus = anti-Dsg1 only + NO mucosal + superficial bullae; Vulgaris = anti-Dsg3 (+Dsg1) + mucosa + deep
Acute urticaria vs angioedemaUrticaria = superficial dermis; Angioedema = deep dermis/subcutis; HAE = no urticaria
Leukoplakia vs lichen planusLP = white reticular/lacy with Wickham's striae; Leukoplakia = homogeneous white patch (no striae)
BCC vs SCCBCC = pearly + telangiectatic border + rarely metastasises; SCC = indurated + everted edge + can metastasize
Condyloma acuminata vs condyloma lataAcuminata = HPV (cauliflower); Lata = secondary syphilis (flat, moist, smooth)
Mycosis fungoides vs psoriasisMF = Pautrier's microabscesses (atypical lymphocytes); Psoriasis = Munro's microabscesses (neutrophils)
